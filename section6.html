Section 6: Numerical Solution of the Wave Equation

We will be disscussing the method of finding the numerical solution of the wave euqation, which is called Central in Time Central in Space or CTCS.

In this section, there are two topics to be studied:

  • Discretisation of the Equation
  • Solve the Algorithm

    • Discretisation of the Equation

    We have studied the numerical solution foe heat euqation. We can do the similar thing to the wave euqation to obtain the numerical solution. Before we start finding the solution, we need to keep the following three criteria in mind:

    1. It must be accurate enough for your needs;
    2. It must be consistent;
    3. It must be stable;
    Only by fullfilling the above conditions, we can be confident to derive a numerical solution for the equation.

    Derivation

    The governing euqation is: $$ u_{tt} - c^2 u_{xx} = 0 $$ By Taylor formula we can obatin the following approximation of \(u_{tt}\) and \(u_{xx} \). $$ \begin{align} u^{n}_{i+1} &= u(x + \Delta x,t) \\ u^{n}_{i-1} &= u(x - \Delta x,t) \\ u^{n+1}_{i} &= u(x,t + \Delta t) \\ u^{n-1}_{i} &= u(x,t - \Delta t) \end{align} $$ Thus, by Taylor's formula:

    (1) can be expressed as: \begin{align*} u^{n}_{i+1} &= u(x + \Delta x,t)\\ &= u(x,t)+ \Delta x u_x(x,t) + \frac{1}{2} (\Delta x)^2 u_{xx}(x,t) + \frac{1}{6} (\Delta x)^3 u_{xxx}(x,t) + \frac{1}{24} (\Delta x)^4 u_{xxxx}(x,t)+... \tag{a} \end{align*} (2) can be expressed as: \begin{align*} u^{n}_{i-1} &= u(x - \Delta x,t)\\ &= u(x,t)- \Delta x u_x(x,t) + \frac{1}{2} (\Delta x)^2 u_{xx}(x,t) - \frac{1}{6} (\Delta x)^3 u_{xxx}(x,t) + \frac{1}{24} (\Delta x)^4 u_{xxxx}(x,t)+... \tag{b} \end{align*} (3) can be expressed as: \begin{align*} u^{n+1}_{i} &= u(x,t + \Delta t)\\ &= u(x,t)+ \Delta t u_t(x,t) + \frac{1}{2} (\Delta t)^2 u_{tt}(x,t) + \frac{1}{6} (\Delta t)^3 u_{ttt}(x,t) + \frac{1}{24} (\Delta t)^4 u_{tttt}(x,t)+... \tag{c} \end{align*} (4) can be expressed as: \begin{align*} u^{n-1}_{i} &= u(x,t - \Delta t)\\ &= u(x,t)- \Delta t u_t(x,t) + \frac{1}{2} (\Delta t)^2 u_{tt}(x,t) - \frac{1}{6} (\Delta t)^3 u_{ttt}(x,t) + \frac{1}{24} (\Delta t)^4 u_{tttt}(x,t)+... \tag{d} \end{align*} By adding (a) and (b), we can obtain: \begin{align*} u^{n}_{i+1} + u^{n}_{i-1} &= 2 u(x,t) + (\Delta x)^2 u_{xx}(x,t) + \frac{1}{12} (\Delta x)^4 u_{xxxx}(x,t) + ... \\ \Rightarrow u_{xx}(x,t) &= \frac{u^{n}_{i+1} + u^{n}_{i-1} - 2 u(x,t)}{(\Delta x)^2} - \frac{(\Delta x)^2}{12}u_{xxxx}(x,t) - ... \tag{2a} \end{align*} By adding (c) and (d), we can obtain: \begin{align*} u^{n+1}_{i} + u^{n-1}_{i} &= 2 u(x,t) + (\Delta t)^2 u_{tt}(x,t) + \frac{1}{12} (\Delta t)^4 u_{tttt}(x,t) + ... \\ \Rightarrow u_{tt}(x,t) &= \frac{u^{n+1}_{i} + u^{n-1}_{i-1} - 2 u(x,t)}{(\Delta t)^2} - \frac{(\Delta t)^2}{12}u_{tttt}(x,t) - ... \tag{2b} \end{align*} Which gives: $$ \frac{u^{n+1}_{i} + u^{n-1}_{i-1} - 2 u(x,t)}{(\Delta t)^2} - c^2 \frac{u^{n}_{i+1} + u^{n}_{i-1} - 2 u(x,t)}{(\Delta x)^2} = 0 $$ By rearanging the above expression, we can obtain: $$ u_{i}^{n+1} = 2u_i^n - u_i^{n-1} + \frac{\Delta t^2 c^2}{\Delta x^2}(u_{i+1}^n-2u_i^n+u_{i-1}^n) $$

    Accuracy:

    The truncation error is \(O(\Delta t^2, \Delta x^2)\) which is acceptable.

    Consistency:

    As \(\Delta x and \Delta t -> \infty\) the euqation 2a and 2b will be the derivative. Thus, it is consistent.

    Stability:

    To disscuss the stability of the numerical solution, we need to use Von Neumann Stability Analysis, which first take the form of: $$ U_i^n = G^n e^{IiL\Delta x} $$ Thus, we can have: $$ u_i^{n+1} = 2 u_i^n - u_i^{n-1} + \alpha ^2 (u_{i+1}^n -2 u_i^n +u_{i-1}^n) \quad \text{where} \quad \alpha^2 = \frac{\Delta t^2 c^2}{\Delta x^2} $$ By sunstitution and division (the factor is: \(G^{n-1}e^{Iik\Delta x}\)): $$ G^2 = 2G -1 + \alpha^2 (Ge^{Ik\Delta x} - 2G + Ge^{-Ik\Delta x}) \\ \therefore \quad G^2 = 2G(1-\alpha^2) - 1 + 2 \alpha^2 G cos(k\Delta x) \\ \Rightarrow \quad G^2 = 2 G [1- \alpha^2 (1- cos{(k\Delta x)})]-1 $$ We can use the trig identity to make a transform: $$ \because \quad 1 - cos{k\Delta x}= 2sin^2{(\frac{k\Delta x}{2})} \\ \Rightarrow \quad G^2 - \beta G + 1 = 0 \quad \text{where} \quad \beta = 1 - 2\alpha^2 sin^2(\frac{k\Delta x}{2}) $$

    So our solution now is \(\quad G^2 - \beta G + 1 = 0 \quad \text{where} \quad \beta = 1 - 2\alpha^2 sin^2(\frac{k\Delta x}{2})\). It is in the form of a quadratic euqation, we can solve it: $$ G = \beta \pm \sqrt{\beta^2 -1} $$ If \(\mid \beta \mid > 1\) \(\Rightarrow\) \(\mid G \mid \geq 1\) which means it is unstable. We want it be stable that is \(\mid \beta \mid \leq 1\) that is \(-1 \leq \beta \leq 1\). $$ -1 \leq 1-2\alpha^2sin^2{\frac{k\Delta x}{2}} \leq 1 $$ As \(sin^2{\frac{k\Delta x}{2}} \in [0,1]\), because 0 is trivial: $$ -1 \leq 1-2\alpha^2 \leq 1 \\ 0 \leq -2 \alpha^2 \leq 0 \\ 0 \leq \alpha^2 \leq 1 \\ \therefore \mid \alpha \mid \leq 1 \\ \because \alpha^2 =\frac{\Delta t^2 c^2}{\Delta x^2} \Rightarrow \alpha \quad \text{is non-negative} \\ \Rightarrow 0 \leq \alpha \leq1 \\ \Rightarrow 0 \leq \frac{c\Delta t}{\Delta x}\leq 1 $$ Thus, our time step is limited by: $$ \frac{c\Delta t}{\Delta x}\leq 1 $$

    In reality, we set the time step size as follow: $$ \Delta t = CFL \frac{\Delta x}{c} $$ Where CFL is the Courant-Friedrichs-Lewy condition, and for this shceme \(CFL\leq1\) is required for stability.

    So now, we have a scheme that is:

    • Accurate
    • Consistent
    • Stable

    Solve the Algorithm

    In order to solve the scheme, we need to find out our time step at 'n' and 'n-1'. But, at the begining, how can we assign '0' to '0-1'? We can have two ways:

    1. \(u_i^{0-1} = u^n_i\), thus at time step 1: \(u_i^{0-1}=u_i^0\)
    2. \(u_t(x,0) = g(x)\)
